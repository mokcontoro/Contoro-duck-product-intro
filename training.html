Contoro at a Glance

Our Technology

This is our Autonomous Trailer Unloading Robot — also known as the Dock Duck. It automates one of the most physically demanding tasks in warehouse logistics.

What Do We Do?

We automate offloading floor-loaded trailers. The Duck enters the container, picks up boxes one at a time, and places them onto the conveyor system for palletization.

Why This Matters

Reduces injury risk for associates and allows them to work on safer tasks.

Keeps associates inside the warehouse and out of extremely hot or cold containers.

Module 3

Safety & Risk Awareness

Why Safety Matters

The Duck system includes powerful industrial equipment. Key safety concerns:

  1. High Voltage — The system operates at 480V which can be fatal
  2. Heavy Moving Parts — Conveyors and drive mechanisms can cause crush injuries
  3. Powerful Robotic Arm — The KUKA arm can move suddenly with great force

Things to Pay Attention To

  1. Status Indicator Light — Always check the light color before approaching
  2. KUKA Robotic Arm — Can move suddenly with force
  3. High Voltage — Never open electrical panels
  4. Warehouse Machinery — Be aware of forklifts and other equipment
  5. Robot Emergency Stops — Know where all 6 E-stops are located

The Status Indicator Light

Container Unloading Situations

  1. Robot Arm crashes into wall — Press E-Stop immediately
  2. Robot stops / Gets stuck — Report via UI, contact Contoro staff
  3. Someone entering the container — Press E-Stop immediately
  4. Unexpected movement — Press E-Stop immediately
Module 4

Operation Modes & Behaviors

Robot Status Modes

The Duck has four states, indicated by the Status Indicator Light:

Red = E-Stop

  • Robot cannot move
  • Safe to approach
  • E-Stop engaged

Blue = Reset

  • Requesting reset
  • Cannot move
  • Press reset to change state

Yellow = Manual

  • Drivable with Xbox Controller
  • Arm can be moved manually
  • Safety Cart cannot control

Green = Ready/Active

  • NOT safe to approach
  • Arm can move
  • Ready to unload

Manual Driving

In Yellow (Manual) mode, drive the Duck with an Xbox Controller. Used to move the Duck from storage to the container and back.

Setup Procedure

1

Safety Cart

Position at the designated operator station

2

Robot / Duck

Drive to container opening using Xbox controller (Yellow mode)

3

Conveyors

Connect powered and flex conveyors to palletization area

4

Safety Fence

Deploy fence and ensure all interlocks engaged

Setup Demo

Teardown Procedure

After unloading is complete, tear down in reverse order:

1

Safety Fence

Retract and stow

2

Conveyors

Disconnect and collapse

3

Robot / Duck

Drive back to storage (Yellow mode)

4

Safety Cart

Return to storage position

Operating the Duck

Full Operation Workflow

Setup

II

Assign Container

Choose Facility and Container ID in the UI

III

Start Robot

Click Green Start Button on the Safety Cart

IV

Monitor & Respond

Pause from UI, Reset from Safety Cart if Blue, E-Stop for emergencies

V

Unloading Finished

Robot auto-detects empty container and stops

VI

Teardown

Reverse setup: fence, conveyors, Duck to storage, cart

When to Press E-Stop During Unloading

  1. Crash — Robot arm hits container wall or structure
  2. Incident — Any unexpected behavior or malfunction
  3. Entering the container — Anyone enters while robot is active

After E-Stop: Fill out the Incident Report form in the UI (choose tag, add notes, click Report).
